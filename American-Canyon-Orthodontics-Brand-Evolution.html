01 — Honest Assessment

Your brand is working
against you.

I want to be direct with you — because that's what $2,000/hour buys. Your current logo and brand identity have the warmth and community spirit that clearly matters to your practice. The mountain imagery, the "Building Smiles for a Lifetime" tagline — these show heart.

But the execution reads as early 2000s clip-art. The gradient mountains, the competing orange and blue, the dated script font — they signal a practice that hasn't evolved. And I know that's not true.

You have two accomplished doctors building something lasting. Your brand needs to match that ambition.

What I see in the current brand

Gradient Mountains

Reads as stock imagery, not custom identity

Color Clash

Orange + blue fight each other instead of harmonizing

Script Tagline

Casual script undermines clinical credibility

No Hierarchy

Everything competes for attention, nothing leads

Two Doctor Names

Listed equally but no visual system to unify them

Scalability

Logo breaks at small sizes — favicon, social, embroidery

"The bones are good — the mountain connection, the warmth, the community feel. We're not starting over. We're elevating."

07 — The Verdict

Should you change the name?

No. "American Canyon Orthodontics" is a powerful name. It's geographic, it's memorable, and it anchors you to your community. The problem was never the name — it was the visual execution.

What I've shown you today is a brand that takes everything that was right about your identity — the warmth, the mountain connection, the community spirit — and elevates it to match the caliber of care you actually deliver.
